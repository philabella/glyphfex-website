What actually breaks in a sign shop

Every signage forum thread surfaces the same three problems. GlyphFex attacks all three.

"We under-quote the hard stuff"

Channel letters with stud-mount pattern onto EIFS takes 3x the labor of flush-mount on wood. Your estimator's gut forgets. Smart Quoting tags every job with its mounting, substrate, illumination — future quotes with the same combination surface the real median hours.

"The installer didn't log time"

The Shop Floor terminal has one giant "Start" button per job. Forgot? Tap the start time, backdate to when you actually started — no supervisor phone call needed. Forgot to stop? Next login catches overnight timers and offers a shift-end rollback so Friday's job doesn't silently log 72 hrs. Edits show as an amber "edited" pill on the timesheet — self-service for the installer, full visibility for you.

"Install-day drive time isn't free"

Three stops across the metro on a Tuesday install run? One tap on the Travel button separates windshield time from hands-on installation. Your weekly hours card shows it as a split: "28.5 work · 4.3 travel". Bill drive at a different rate, deduct it from shop-productive, or just see where Tuesday went.

"Permits and installs live in my head"

Custom built-in fields track permit number, site survey date, install window, engineering stamp. Gantt calendar shows every job by install date. Shop-floor Display Board on a TV so the crew sees today's installs when they walk in.
